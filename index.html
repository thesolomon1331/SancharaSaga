A fascinating aspect about the temples in Karnataka is that they belong to different dynasties making each of them unique in terms of religious context, architectural styles, and historical significance.

The Jog Falls has the distinction of being called the 'Niagara of India' and is one of the highest and most beautiful waterfalls in the country.

The Wildlife Sanctuaries in Karnataka are home to a large variety of animals, like the elephant, sloth bears, tigers, jackals, the gaur and so many more. Having a forest area of almost 38,720 square kilometres, more than 20 percent of Karnataka is under forest cover.

The dynamic presence of the Western Ghats in Karnataka makes it the best destination for trekkers, nature lovers, photographers, and wildlife enthusiasts.
